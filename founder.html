ABOUT THE MAHARAJA SAYAJIRAO UNIVERSITY OF BARODA

India's internationally renowned, The Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda (1949) has blossomed from the Baroda College (1881) which was one of the oldest centre of learning in Western India. MSU is the only state University with a residential, unitary character having English as medium of instruction with more than 1200 well-qualified faculty members and 1500 administrative staff members to facilitate the learning of more than 35000 students on campus. Spread on 275 acres of land, it comprises of 14 faculties having 111 departments, 3 constituent colleges and several specialized centers and institutes offering a wide spectrum of courses. The University is accredited ’A+’ by NAAC.
